I am a doctoral researcher in Computer Science and Engineering at the [University of Oulu](https://www.oulu.fi/), Finland, where I work at the intersection of **urban well-being**, **machine learning**, and **graph neural networks**. My research focuses on data-driven analysis of smart-city systems using multi-source urban data, with the goal of building models that are accurate, robust, and useful for understanding how cities affect the people who live in them.

Before starting my PhD, I worked for several years as a computer-vision and deep-learning researcher in industry, designing algorithms for automatic number-plate recognition, weigh-in-motion systems, and intelligent surveillance — and helping deploy these systems at scale. I hold an M.Sc. in Digital Electronic Systems from [Sharif University of Technology](http://www.en.sharif.edu/) and a B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from [Amirkabir University of Technology](https://www.aut.ac.ir/en).

My current work spans spatio-temporal graph learning, traffic and mobility modeling, and broader questions around how heterogeneous urban data (sensing, environmental, social) can be combined to study well-being in cities. I am always happy to discuss research, collaboration, or interesting datasets — feel free to reach out by [email](mailto:amirhossein.ghaffari@oulu.fi).
